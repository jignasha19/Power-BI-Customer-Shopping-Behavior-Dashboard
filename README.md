# Power-BI-Customer-Shopping-Behavior-Dashboard

## 📊 Project Insights 

* **Revenue Growth:** Revenue increased from **$98,871 to $233,081** (**+135.74%**), showing strong business growth.
* **Customer Segments:** Very loyal customers bring in most revenue. Loyal, regular, and occasional customers are growing fast. New customers are an opportunity to gain more sales.
* **Products & Categories:** Top revenue comes from **Blouse, Shirt, Dress, Pants, and Jewelry**. **Clothing and Accessories** make most of the revenue, while Footwear and Outerwear contribute less.
* **Subscription Model:** ~**75% of customers are non-subscribers**, and ~25% are subscribers. There is a big opportunity to convert non-subscribers into recurring buyers.
* **Size Distribution:**

  * **Medium (M)** is the most popular size (46.58%).
  * **M + L** together make up 72.41% of total sales — the main sizes to focus on.
  * **Small (S) and XL** are lower in demand and should be stocked carefully.
* **Discount Impact:** Targeted discounts influence **57.43% of customers**, driving sales while 42.57% remain unaffected.
* **Geography:** Most revenue comes from **North America**, especially **California, Texas, New York, and Florida**. Smaller states like Vermont, Wyoming, North Dakota, and South Dakota have fewer customers.
* **Customer Spending:** Average order value is **$59.76**. **68% of revenue comes from male customers**, highlighting potential to target female buyers more.
* **Growth Drivers:** Revenue growth is supported by **customer retention, subscription promotion, discounts, inventory planning, and focus on mid-range sizes (M & L)**.

**🎯 Overall Conclusion:**
Revenue is growing strongly across all customer segments. Very loyal customers and mid-range sizes (M & L) drive most sales. Clothing and Accessories are the key revenue categories. Converting non-subscribers, targeting female customers, and carefully managing smaller sizes can further boost performance.
